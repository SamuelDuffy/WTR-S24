Goals

My goals pertained to the skills required for good documentation. After my first tasks, I decided that good documentation could be achieved through the following three paths:

Content - Written Form

From previous examples, I realized that all good documentation strikes a balance between detail/specificity and conciseness/readability. I read over examples given to me by my supervisor, reputable online examples, and various other sources to try to "feel-out" the golden mean.

Style - Visual Form

The other "form" of documentation was its literal "look". Some documentation was segmented into spaced blocks representing "steps". Others structured as one continuous string of actions, letting the user simply follow along. Even seemingly minute details like bolding, font size, font, could greatly improve or impair the flow of the page.
