Linear Algebra

Although taught during my first year, I never truly aquainted myself with Linear Algebra. The temporary professor responsible for teaching the course was nothing less than horrid, and a petition requesting their removal recieved more than 2/3rd's of the class's signature. I decided to give the subject another chance, and carefully watched and rewatched 3Blue1Brown's 16-video course on Linear algebra. After watching this course, and deeply researching any parts that didn't immediatly "click", I felt I had gained a true and deep intuition for all of the basics of Linear Algebra, inlcuding vectors, matrices, spans, dot and cross products, determinants, eigenvectors and eigenvalues, change of base, and many other topics covered in the series. After I had established this base, I moved on to learning the slightly more advanced concepts such as Spectral Decomposition and Singular Value Decomposition.

Calculus

After the shocking ease with which I was able to pick up Linear Alegrba, I figured I would watch 3Blue1Brown's series on Calculus. The course deeply covered derivatives and many of their rules, integrals, the formal definition of the limit, higher order derivatives, integrals, and taylor series. Blag blah blah
